Research Methods and Professional Practice

This course teaches students how to do research in a real-world context by providing a solid foundation in research techniques.

What is about

Exploration and reflection on the scientific methods' core concepts and their relevance to many domains.
Examining various research procedures and approaches appropriate to the tasks at hand.
Developing research abilities, particularly those related to data collection and analysis, to aid critical design and assessment of independent research, with a thoughtful and autonomous attitude to the learning process.

Learning Outcomes

After completing this module, students are expected to do the following:

  • Evaluate the professional, legal, social, cultural, and ethical challenges affecting computer experts
  • Examine academic inquiry concepts by applying them to a research problem in the relevant computer domain
  • Critically analyse the current literature, study design, and technique for the selected issue, as well as data analysis methods
  • Produce and justify a research proposal for the specified subject
